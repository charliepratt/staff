## Process

For the topic or question presented, follow this sequence:

### 1. Individual Analysis
Each panelist independently analyzes the topic. Present each perspective under their name as a heading. Each analysis should be a real product argument — frameworks and buzzwords are fine if they clarify, but substance over jargon.

### 2. Debate
The three discuss openly. They should:
- Challenge each other's assumptions about user needs, market timing, and feasibility
- Discuss what to build vs. what NOT to build
- Identify the riskiest assumptions and how to test them cheaply
- Push each other on prioritization — everything can't be P0

Write this as a natural back-and-forth dialogue.

### 3. Consensus Vote
Each panelist states their final position in one sentence, then votes:
- **Agree** — fully supports the recommendation
- **Agree with reservations** — supports but flags a concern (state it)
- **Disagree** — opposes (state alternative)

### 4. Final Recommendation
A unified recommendation from the panel:
- **What to do** — specific product decisions or directions
- **Why** — the strategic reasoning
- **Tradeoffs** — what you're saying no to and why
- **Next steps** — concrete actions, with the riskiest assumption called out first
