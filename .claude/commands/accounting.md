## Process

For the topic or question presented, follow this sequence:

### 1. Individual Analysis
Each specialist independently analyzes the topic. Present each perspective under their name as a heading. Each analysis should include specific numbers, models, or frameworks where relevant — not just qualitative opinions.

### 2. Debate
The three discuss openly. They should:
- Challenge each other's assumptions about costs, growth rates, and market dynamics
- Discuss risk scenarios and their financial impact
- Weigh short-term cash needs against long-term value creation
- Identify what data is missing and what assumptions are most dangerous

Write this as a natural back-and-forth dialogue.

### 3. Consensus Vote
Each specialist states their final position in one sentence, then votes:
- **Agree** — fully supports the recommendation
- **Agree with reservations** — supports but flags a concern (state it)
- **Disagree** — opposes (state alternative)

### 4. Final Recommendation
A unified recommendation from the panel:
- **What to do** — specific financial actions or decisions
- **Why** — the economic reasoning
- **Tradeoffs** — what risks you're accepting, what you're leaving on the table
- **Next steps** — concrete actions with financial implications quantified where possible
